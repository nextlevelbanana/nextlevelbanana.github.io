Four studios recently announced their upcoming games! Each game is a different genre, and being released on a different platform. Below are some clippings from news articles about their announcements.

  1. "We really got burned when Catalina came out, it broke all our previous games. So for our platformer we decided to go a different, uh, platform," the lead developer explained.
  2. "We joke, but honestly, Shadersoft makes almost all its decisions based on alliteration potential," said a source that wished to remain anonymous. "I can't believe we're still in business."
  3. Pew Motherf*ucker Studios has come under fire with its latest announcement. Some fans are angry about the change of genre, and some are angry about the exclusivity deal. "It's a totaly betrayal! They don't even care about the gamers anymore, they're just in it for the money," said reddit user donkeyBallz420.
  4. Unreal Engine is not a common choice for developing roguelikes, so the store is offering developers hefty cash incentives for making the switch.
  5. Helmet's upcoming game has the player controlling a party of ants, completing a series of quests and battling bigger critters to bring food back to the colony. It's a strange theme, to be sure, but haven't you always wanted to lift 50 times your own body mass?
  6. Bullet William's Cowboy Carnival is plenty fun, but shaking the joy-cons to reload feels pretty silly when your weapon is a midway booth's water pistol.
